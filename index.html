Engaging and Active Security Education (EASE) workshop

Security is both an interesting and fascinating topic, but it is often challenging to deliver engaging security education that actively involves all students. An approach that engages some students, such as attacking a system, may disengage others. Further still, a practice, such as designing an authentication interfaces, may excite some and infuriate others. The concern is that some approaches, engaging or not, may be turning students away from area of cyber security. The situation is only further exacerbated with the present pandemic with many activities having to move to remote delivery across the world. Consequently, the focus of this workshop is to share and discuss engage and activity teaching practice for cyber security education.
